By using the Colorado Tree Coalition Notable Tree App you agree to the following terms:

The user will be respectful to private property owners when viewing trees on private property. Trees on private property shall be viewed from the abutting/adjacent public sidewalk or street, unless permission is obtained from the property owner first.

Public and/or private notable trees and surrounding property shall not be tampered with in any way and left as they were found. Examples of tampering include but are not solely defined as; branch cuttings, leaf pulling/collecting, bark cutting, collecting acorns or seeds from the tree, and writing and carving onto the tree in any way.

The user shall adhere to the legal use of public right of way, i.e.; no illegal parking, blocking of walking paths/sidewalks, or impeding traffic when viewing notable trees. The user should pay attention to personal safety when viewing the notable trees. The trees and property owners thank you!
